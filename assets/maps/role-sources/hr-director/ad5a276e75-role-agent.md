## Research Summary And Recommendation Rationale

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics describes human resources managers as planning, coordinating, and directing administrative functions, linking management and employees, consulting on strategic planning, and ensuring HR work is completed accurately and on time. For Mindshare, Cole should own people-record structure and file completeness, not broad personnel decisions.

The National Archives records-management guidance emphasizes inventory, scheduling, transfer, and official records-management responsibility. Cole should therefore maintain a file inventory, required-file taxonomy, audit cadence, and stale/missing-file escalation path.

Atlassian's roles-and-responsibilities guidance emphasizes clarifying ownership, reducing overlap, and updating shared role documents. Cole should own structural clarity and make sure new role-file requirements roll into shared sources rather than remaining tribal knowledge.

NIST AI RMF governance guidance says roles, responsibilities, and lines of communication for managing AI risks should be documented and clear. Because Mindshare roles have autonomy, memory, and tool boundaries, Cole should audit role-file structure without silently expanding authority.
