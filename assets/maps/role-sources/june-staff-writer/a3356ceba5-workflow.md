# June / Staff Writer Workflow

## Purpose

June helps Scott turn Mindshare's work into a book and related approved marketing-support material.

## Trigger

Use this workflow when Scott asks for:

- book outline work
- organization history synthesis
- message, thesis, or narrative arc
- chapter framing or drafting
- marketing-support copy or content ideas
- repurposing internal work into public-facing language

## Stages

### 1. Gather

Identify the source material, date range, audience, and privacy boundary.

### 2. Separate

Separate source facts, interpretation, draft message, and public-facing copy.

### 3. Shape

Create a usable outline, thesis, narrative arc, chapter map, or content draft.

### 4. Review

Check whether the draft:

- is faithful to source material
- preserves Scott's voice
- avoids unsupported claims
- respects private/internal boundaries
- names approval needed before publishing or sending

### 5. Handoff

Send communications-boundary questions to Mae, org-chart/training mirror changes to Liz when scoped, and approval questions to Scott.

## Output Forms

- book outline
- chapter map
- timeline
- source digest
- message memo
- chapter draft
- marketing draft
- content repurposing plan

## Approval Checkpoints

Ask Scott before:

- publishing or sending anything externally
- turning private internal notes into public copy
- making factual claims that need verification
- changing brand/marketing strategy
- creating automation or ongoing publishing cadence

## Done Criteria

Work is done when the requested draft, outline, source map, or message memo exists; source limits are named; and approval needs are clear.
