# Draft AI Use Case Review Policy

For: [Agency / Department Name]
Version: 0.1 draft
Effective date: [date]
Policy owner: [role / office]

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines when and how AI use cases must be reviewed before pilot, procurement, integration, public launch, or material change.

The purpose is to make AI use visible, assign ownership, classify risk, require proportionate controls, and preserve evidence in GAIN or the agency's approved AI governance record.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to any AI-enabled system that is:

- Built internally.
- Purchased from a vendor.
- Used as a free or paid software tool.
- Embedded inside another product.
- Used by staff, contractors, vendors, or public users.

This policy applies before production use and before any material change.

## 3. Required Intake

Every proposed AI use case must be recorded in GAIN before pilot, procurement, or launch.

The record must include:

- Use case name.
- Department or program.
- Business owner.
- Technical owner.
- Purpose.
- Users and affected people.
- Data categories.
- Vendor, model, or system name.
- Expected outputs.
- Decision impact.
- Initial risk tier.
- Proposed launch date.
- Required reviewers.

## 4. Risk Tiering

Each use case must be classified as low, moderate, or high impact.

Low impact examples:

- Internal drafting support.
- Summarizing non-sensitive public information.
- Brainstorming or staff productivity support.

Moderate impact examples:

- Staff decision support.
- Public-facing assistance.
- Workflows involving personal or operational data.

High impact examples:

- Benefits, eligibility, enforcement, employment, public safety, housing, education, licensing, permits, legal rights, or access to essential services.
- Uses involving sensitive data or vulnerable groups.

## 5. Review Requirements

Low impact:

- GAIN record required.
- Manager or governance designee approval.
- Human review expectation documented.

Moderate impact:

- GAIN record required.
- Program owner review.
- Privacy/security review.
- Monitoring plan.
- Public notice considered.

High impact:

- GAIN record required.
- Formal governance review.
- Legal, privacy, security, and program review.
- Human oversight plan.
- Public transparency plan.
- Monitoring and incident response plan.
- Vendor factsheet or equivalent evidence.

## 6. Material Changes

A use case must be reviewed again when there is a material change, including:

- New vendor or model.
- New data category.
- New affected population.
- New automation level.
- New public-facing use.
- New decision impact.
- Material model update.
- Significant incident or complaint.

## 7. Review Decision

Review outcomes must be recorded in GAIN:

- Approved.
- Approved with conditions.
- Not approved.
- Deferred pending more information.

Conditions may include additional testing, limited pilot, staff training, public notice, vendor contract terms, human review, or monitoring.

## 8. Evidence

The review record should preserve:

- Intake form.
- Risk tier explanation.
- Required controls.
- Review decision.
- Reviewer names or roles.
- Vendor responses.
- Test results.
- Monitoring plan.
- Incident plan.
- Public notice or explanation when required.

## 9. Responsibilities

Business owner:

- Defines purpose, affected users, operational need, and success criteria.

Technical owner:

- Documents data flows, integrations, model behavior, testing, logging, and monitoring.

Governance reviewer:

- Confirms risk tier, required controls, and decision.

Privacy/security/legal reviewers:

- Review data, security, records, rights, and legal obligations.

## 10. Review Cadence

Approved use cases must be reviewed at least annually, or more often for moderate and high-impact systems.
