## 1. Purpose

This policy defines when human review, approval, escalation, or appeal is required for AI-supported work.

AI systems may support staff, but people remain responsible for agency decisions, communications, and public service outcomes.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to AI systems that draft, recommend, classify, summarize, predict, answer questions, automate tasks, or support decisions.

It applies to internal and public-facing systems.

## 3. Oversight Principles

Human oversight must be:

- Meaningful: reviewers have enough information and authority to change the outcome.
- Proportionate: higher-risk uses require stronger oversight.
- Documented: responsibilities and decisions are recorded.
- Timely: review happens before harm or final action when required.
- Accessible: affected people have a way to ask for human review when appropriate.

## 4. Oversight Levels

Level 1: Human checks before use

- AI output is used for drafting or brainstorming.
- Staff reviews before relying on it.

Level 2: Human approves before action

- AI output supports service delivery or staff decisions.
- A designated reviewer approves before the output affects a person.

Level 3: Human decision required

- AI output may inform but may not determine a final decision.
- Final decision must be made by authorized staff.

Level 4: Enhanced oversight

- High-impact systems require documented review, escalation, monitoring, appeal/correction path, and periodic audit.

## 5. Required Oversight by Risk

Low impact:

- Staff review AI output before use.
- No final decision made solely by AI.

Moderate impact:

- Reviewer role identified.
- Review criteria documented.
- Escalation path defined.
- Monitoring owner assigned.

High impact:

- Human decision-maker identified.
- AI may not be the sole final decision-maker unless explicitly approved.
- Appeal or correction process required.
- Review evidence preserved.
- Periodic audit required.

## 6. Reviewer Responsibilities

Reviewers must check:

- Accuracy.
- Completeness.
- Source support.
- Bias or unequal treatment.
- Tone and accessibility.
- Legal or policy consistency.
- Whether the output should be refused, corrected, escalated, or discarded.

## 7. Escalation

Staff must escalate when:

- AI output may affect rights, benefits, enforcement, eligibility, employment, safety, or access to services.
- Output appears biased, unsafe, or materially inaccurate.
- The system behaves unexpectedly.
- A person contests an AI-supported result.
- Staff are unsure whether the use is approved.

## 8. Public Review and Appeal

When AI supports a consequential public-facing process, affected people should be told how to:

- Ask whether AI was used.
- Request correction.
- Request human review.
- Appeal or contest an outcome.
- Contact the agency.

## 9. Documentation

GAIN or the agency record should include:

- Oversight level.
- Reviewer role.
- Review criteria.
- Escalation path.
- Appeal/correction process.
- Evidence retention requirements.
- Monitoring cadence.

## 10. Training

Staff reviewers must be trained on:

- AI limitations.
- Automation bias.
- Data and privacy rules.
- Required review criteria.
- Escalation and incident reporting.
- How to document decisions.
