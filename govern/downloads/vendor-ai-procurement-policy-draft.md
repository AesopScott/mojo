# Draft Vendor AI Procurement Policy

For: [Agency / Department Name]
Version: 0.1 draft
Effective date: [date]
Policy owner: [role / office]

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines what the agency must ask, evaluate, and require when buying, licensing, integrating, or renewing AI-enabled vendor products.

The purpose is to make vendor AI claims testable, contract terms enforceable, and risks visible before adoption.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- New AI procurements.
- Renewals involving AI-enabled products.
- Products where AI is embedded in a larger system.
- Free trials, pilots, proofs of concept, and demos using agency data.
- Vendor systems used by contractors on behalf of the agency.

## 3. Required Pre-Procurement Review

Before procurement or pilot, the team must record the proposed use case in GAIN and complete initial risk tiering.

Procurement should not proceed until required reviewers identify:

- Data categories.
- Decision impact.
- Public-facing use.
- Required vendor disclosures.
- Required contract terms.
- Required testing or pilot conditions.

## 4. Vendor Disclosure Questions

Vendors should answer:

- What AI capabilities are included?
- What model or model type is used?
- What data trained or tunes the model?
- Will agency data be used for training or improvement?
- What prompts, inputs, outputs, and logs are retained?
- Where is data stored?
- Who can access agency data?
- What subprocessors are used?
- What testing has been performed?
- What limitations are known?
- How are model updates handled?
- How will the agency be notified of material changes?
- What audit logs are available?
- How are incidents reported?

## 5. Required Contract Terms

Contracts should address:

- Approved purpose.
- Data ownership.
- Ban or limits on vendor training using agency data.
- Retention and deletion.
- Subprocessor disclosure.
- Security controls.
- Audit rights.
- Incident notification timeline.
- Accessibility requirements.
- Public records and records retention.
- Model change notification.
- Termination and data return/deletion.
- Human oversight support.
- Compliance with applicable law and agency policy.

## 6. Testing and Evaluation

Before launch, vendors may be required to provide or support:

- Accuracy testing.
- Bias or disparate impact testing.
- Security testing.
- Privacy review.
- Red-team or misuse testing.
- Accessibility review.
- User acceptance testing.
- Documentation of known limitations.

Testing evidence should be attached to the GAIN use case record.

## 7. High-Impact Systems

High-impact vendor AI systems require enhanced review before procurement or launch.

Enhanced review may include:

- Legal review.
- Privacy impact assessment.
- Security assessment.
- Public transparency plan.
- Human oversight plan.
- Incident response plan.
- Contractual audit rights.
- Pilot limits before full deployment.

## 8. Vendor Changes

Vendors must notify the agency of material changes, including:

- New model.
- New training data source.
- New data use.
- New subprocessor.
- Material performance change.
- Security incident.
- Change in retention, logging, or training practices.

Material changes may require re-review in GAIN.

## 9. Procurement Record

The procurement record should include:

- GAIN use case ID.
- Risk tier.
- Vendor factsheet responses.
- Required contract terms.
- Review decisions.
- Testing evidence.
- Launch conditions.
- Monitoring owner.
